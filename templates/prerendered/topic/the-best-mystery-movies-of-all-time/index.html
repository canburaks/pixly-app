The Best Mystery Movies of All Time

~ You know what you look like to me, with your good bag and your cheap shoes? You look like a rube. A well-scrubbed, hustling rube with a little taste. Good nutrition's given you some length of bone, but you're not more than one generation from poor white trash, are you, Agent Starling?
Dr. Hannibal Lecter from The Slience of the Lambs

The category of mysterious films often revolves around the solution of a problem, the relationship between the perpetrator of the sequence of mysterious events or the event. Mystery films mainly focus on solving a crime or a puzzle. Early mystery films date back to the silent period of cinema. Sherlock Holmes Baffled (1903) is considered the first example of such detective-mystery films.

 

This is a very broad genre and it is possible to include many sub-genres like; murder mystery movies, detective movies, crime mystery movies, horror mystery movies, mystery thriller movies, drama mystery movies etc.

 

Due to the many sub-genres of mystery films and there are plenty of popular must-see mystery movies, we've decided that it is more appropriate to make an archive-wide list rather than 5-10 top-rated mystery movies list. That's why we named it the best mystery films of all time. Although this is a large movie list, you can narrow your search with corresponding IMDb rating or production year by doing this you can still find, for example, good mystery movies of 2019 or which year do you prefer. 

 

 

Brief Information About The Great Mystery Movies

Note: You can skip this part and see the movies below

This archive-wide best mystery film list includes many great movies you must watch. Let me give brief information about some of those;  

  • The Silence of the Lambs (1991), directed by Jonathan Demme, is a serial killer mystery movie which has won The Big Five Academy Awards (Best Picture, Best Director, Best Actor, Best Actress, Best Screenplay). Until today, there are only three movies that have won these 5 awards together.

 

  • Sidney Lumet's Murder on the Orient Express (1974) is a British mystery film, starring Albert Finney, which is based on the novel of the same name by Agatha Christie has nominated six Academy Awards and won the Best Supporting Actress award with the performance of Ingrid Bergman. Also, Lumet's film The Offence (1972), starring Sean Connery, is one of the films worth watching.

 

  • L.A. Confidential (1997) is one of the best police detective films which set in 1940s Los Angeles Police department and nominated for nine Academy Awards and won two of them. (Best Supporting Actress, Best Adapted Screenplay)

 

  • Christopher Nolan's The Prestige (2006) has 8.5 IMDb rating and one of the best works of this genre.

 

  • Clint Eastwood's Mystic River (2003), starring Sean Penn, Tim Robbins, Kevin Bacon and Laurence Fishburne is also a must-see mystery movie with complex characters and based on a novel.

 

  • Martin Scorsese's Shutter Island (2010), starring Leonardo DiCaprio, is a must-see psychological mystery movie.

 

 

  • Speaking of mystery films, David Fincher has to make room for himself. He has produced many outstanding examples of this genre and almost all movies should be watched if you really like this genre. Some of the Fincher's great movies are; The Game (1997) starring Michael Douglas and Sean Penn, Zodiac (2007) is about Zodiac killer in San Francisco, Gone Girl (2014), The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011) and the great Se7en (1995) movie with 8.6 IMDb rating.
