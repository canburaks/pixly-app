25+ Romantic Comedy Movies of All Time

About The List of Romantic Comedies

This time, we made a list of movies with the best and important romantic comedy movies of all time. 

We carefully selected those movies by considering many factors. Those selection criteria include the popularity of the film, IMDb rating, and the cast performances. Moreover, historical significance, originality, and reviews of the cinema professionals also considered. Romantic films solely targeting youth audiences like high school teen movies are deliberately excluded.

 

What is Romantic Comedy?

The romantic comedy in the cinema, generally considered as a sub-genre of comedy by scholars. The genre is also known as rom-com or lesser-known romedy, which are the linguistically blending two genres. Romantic comedy movies generally focus on romantic stories with a humourous plot that has a happy ending. Some argue that romantic comedy should be classified as a movie with two genres, not a separate new genre. 

 

 

Comedy Genre

Comedy is a major genre and has many sub-genres. The distinctions between those sub-genres are originating from the comedy elements that make a movie funny.

However, We can say that gags, jokes and funny moments are fundamentals for all forms of comedy. Those elements usually appear, either together or alone, on other major genres. Therefore, many comedy hybrids such as musical and horror comedies and subgenres like crime, sports, and romantic comedies exist. 

 

Romance 

Romance films usually focus on searching pure love and/or the struggles of lovers in hard situations. There can be many obstacles to prevent them to come together. Those are being such as physical illnesses, racial or religious discriminations, culture clash or financial problems.

The most obvious difference between romance films and romantic comedy movies that lovers meet at the end of the movie. It is more correct to say that the end of the lovers is not tragic. It is not the case for every romance film.

 

Romantic Comedy Movies: Best of Both World

In contemporary cinema, romantic comedy (rom-com) is a well-established genre with familiar patterns. The films have common messages and assumptions. For example, they assume true love exists and awaits us somewhere outside or true love/romance can overcome all obstacles. 

 

World Cinema

Hollywood is the leader if it comes to production. Due to this, It is expected to be more Hollywood films than any other country. We tried to make this list as diverse as possible but our priority is not the origin of the movie. The list includes only two French and one South Korean romantic films. 

Amélie (2001) which is directed by Jean-Pierre Jeunet has nominated and won many prestigious awards. 

Heartbreaker (2010) a.k.a. L'Arnacœur is another French rom-com starring Romain Duris and Vanessa Paradis.

Historical Context

 

Early Romantic Comedies

 

Early romantic comedy cinema dates back to the great depression era. For example, screwball comedy is a U.S origin sub-genre that is popular during the '30s and early '40s. Screwball comedy films satirize the classic love stories and female character dominates the relationship with the main male character.

One of the best examples is Frank Capra's It Happened One Night (1934). The film is the first that won the five major Academy Awards. Until now, Only three films won those big five awards. Besides, Breakfast At Tiffany's (1961) is also another popular film starring Audrey Hepburn of early romantic comedy.

 

The '80s ~ 2000s Romantic Comedies

The romantic comedy has received the greatest attention after the 1980s due to its revival by Hollywood. The successful and popular films of the period are mostly narrated the heterosexual romance stories.

When Harry Met Sally (1989) starring Meg Ryan and is directed by Rob Reiner. A Sydney Pollack movie Tootsie (1982) has ten Oscar nominations. Gary Marshall's Pretty Woman (1990) starring Julia Roberts and Richard Gere.

Also, we should provide a separate section to  Woody Allen's Annie Hall (1977) which led to other so-called 'nervous romance'.
