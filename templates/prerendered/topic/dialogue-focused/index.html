The Great Movies That Focus on Rich Dialogues

Michelangelo? You know a lot about him. Life's work, political aspirations. Him and the pope.Sexual orientation.The whole works, right? I bet you can't tell me what it smells like in the Sistine Chapel. You never actually stood there and looked up at that beautiful ceiling.Seeing that. But you can't tell me what it feels like to wake up next to a woman... and feel truly happy.Sean McGuire (Good Will Huning, 1997)

Cinema is also a verbal tool as well as a visual one. Some films come to the fore with their rich dialogues without compromising their visual and audio values.There are even some that a whole film is almost exclusively among people in a room. Sometimes the dialogue itself can be very striking, and sometimes dialogues step by step forms a great narrative.

This movie list  focuses primarily on the movies that have rich dialogues and the movies themselves are particularly composed of long dialogues. Some of the movies’ brief topics are here;

 

  1. Perfect Strangers; the friendship of old friends are reviewed.

  2. God on Trial; God will be 'judged' in a concentration camp by the prisoners. 

  3. In The trilogy of Before Sunrise, Before Sunset and Before Midnight which are filmed in 20 years, we witness the dialogue between a woman and a man. 

  4. Mindwalk; a long conversations between a physicist, a poet and a presidential candidate. 

  5. The Seventh Seal; we follow the conversation between a knight and the Death who came for him, and the existentialist perspective of Ingmar Bergman. 

  6. 12 Angry Men have an 8.9 IMDb points examined crime, punishment and prejudice and becomes one of the masterpieces in the history of cinema.
