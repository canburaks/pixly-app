The Best Thought Provoking Movies

What is real. How do you define real? If you're talking about what you can feel, what you can smell, what you can taste and see, then real is simply electrical signals interpreted by your brain.
Morpheus - The Matrix (1999)

As the world's most complex art form, cinema offers us new ideas, new ways of thinking about anything. Thought-provoking movies have deep and very provocative effects on us.Generally, those filmsprovoke us to deeply think about the new ideas that are not familiar to us orforce us to rethink our thoughts. We are extensively usingpreviously defined conceptions to move on rather than thinking about everything in-depth. Thought-provoking films cleverly make us question those previously defined conceptions or our assumptions or the things that are told us. We can say that these thoughtful movies try to show a very different world than the world we perceived.

The best thought-provoking movies that we curate for you examine thoroughly the questions that we have never thought of or paid enough attention to. The list has included various provocative and clevermovies that will astonish you with a wide variety of interesting topics like time travel, mental illness, relationship with an unexpected one, and even the existence of itself.

Let's look at some of those mind-bending movies briefly.

  • The Matrix- The best-known and successful work of theWachowski's, which has benefited much from the literature of philosophy and questioned reality itself from a descartesian point of view, and also touched on the dilemma of freedom and human-centricattitude of humanity towards everything.

  • 12 Angry Men(1957) - A courtroom story on the basis of reasonable doubt, the justice system and the moral values.

  • Fight Club (1999)- ADavid Finchermovie that based onChuck Palahniuk'snovel questions the value system of advertising indoctrinated values of capitalist production mechanisms and the conflicts of them with the Generation X.

  • Interstellar (2014)-Christopher Nolan's science-fiction movie is in complete harmony with the science of physics that leads us to rethink the terms time, space, space-time and relativity.

  • Her (2013)-Spike Jonze's sci-fi movie can be classified ascyberpunk. The movie causes us to rethink classical relationships in a very interesting way in the age of artificial intelligence is filling our needs.

  • American History X (1998)- Very powerful movie that hasa long-lasting effectwhich narrates racism and directed byTony Kaye.

  • The Experiment (2001)-Oliver Hirschbiegel's movie is based on a true story of a shocking psychology experiment, known asStanford Prison Experiment, which was held on 1971 by a group of researcher-led byProfessor Philip Zimbardo.The experiment itself reveals a wild side of human nature in a scientific way and caused to start ethics discussions about the psychology experiments in reality.
